How to Fix Internet Dropping Frequently

‚úÖ Quick Answer

If your internet keeps dropping frequently, the problem is usually caused by router firmware issues, weak Wi-Fi signal, loose cables, or ISP instability. Fix it by restarting the router, updating firmware, checking cables, switching frequency band, and resetting network settings.

‚ùì Why Does Internet Drop Frequently?

Frequent internet drops happen when your device loses connection to the router or the router loses connection to the internet. This can happen due to hardware issues, outdated firmware, Wi-Fi interference, or ISP problems.

In many cases, the Wi-Fi signal is weak due to distance or walls, or the router overheating causes frequent restarts. Sometimes, your ISP may be throttling or facing outages.

üîß Fix 1: Restart Router and Modem

This is the easiest and most effective fix. Restarting clears temporary glitches and reconnects the devices.

  1. Turn off your PC/phone.
  2. Unplug the router and modem from power.
  3. Wait 60 seconds.
  4. Plug the modem back in first, wait 2 minutes.
  5. Plug the router back in and wait another 2 minutes.
  6. Turn on your device and check the connection.

üîß Fix 2: Check Router Cables & Ports

Loose or damaged cables cause frequent drops. Check all connections carefully.

  • Check the Ethernet cable between modem and router.
  • Check the power cable for damage or loose connection.
  • If using Ethernet to PC, try a new cable.
  • Try a different LAN port on the router.

üîß Fix 3: Update Router Firmware

Outdated firmware can cause instability and frequent disconnects. Updating firmware can fix bugs.

  1. Open router settings in browser (usually 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1).
  2. Login with admin credentials.
  3. Go to Firmware Update or System.
  4. Click Check for Updates and install.
  5. Restart router after update.

üîß Fix 4: Switch Wi-Fi Band (2.4GHz / 5GHz)

Many routers support both 2.4GHz and 5GHz. Switching bands can stabilize the connection.

  • 2.4GHz: better range, more interference.
  • 5GHz: faster speed, less range, less interference.
  • If your device is far from router, use 2.4GHz.
  • If you have interference, use 5GHz.

üîß Fix 5: Reset Network Settings

Resetting network settings fixes corrupted network configuration and IP conflicts.

  1. Open Settings ‚Üí Network & Internet ‚Üí Status.
  2. Scroll down and click Network reset.
  3. Click Reset now and restart PC.
