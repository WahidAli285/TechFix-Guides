How to Fix Browser Redirecting to Wrong Websites

‚úÖ Quick Answer

Browser redirects to unwanted websites usually happen due to malware, adware, corrupted extensions, or misconfigured browser settings. To fix it, remove malicious software, reset your browser, clear cache and cookies, disable suspicious extensions, and check DNS settings.

‚ùì Why Does My Browser Redirect to Wrong Websites?

Redirect issues occur when your browser is hijacked by malware, malicious extensions, or improper configuration. Other causes include outdated browsers, DNS issues, and some free software that install adware without clear consent. These redirects may lead to unsafe websites, so fixing them quickly is important.

  3. Quarantine or remove any threats found.
  4. Restart your PC and check if redirects stop.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my browser keep redirecting even after clearing cache?

If redirects persist, your system may be infected with malware or adware. Perform a full system scan and check for malicious extensions.

Are browser redirects dangerous?

Yes, they can lead to phishing, malware, or scam websites. Avoid clicking on suspicious links and fix the issue promptly.

Can resetting the browser fix redirects?

Yes, resetting restores default settings and removes malicious configurations, which often stops unwanted redirects.
